Our Story

God Has Been Faithful

Before we talk about where we're going, it's important to know where we've been. The Rock Church's story is one of God's extraordinary faithfulness — through growth, through difficulty, and into what He's building now.

~1998
Founded
The Rock Church launches in Scarborough as a church plant. From the beginning, God's hand is on this place.
2010s
A Season of Growth
The church grows into a multi-service congregation. A building campaign launches. Hundreds of lives are changed.
2020 – 2024
Through the Difficult Years
Like many churches, The Rock faced significant challenges. Leadership changed. COVID reshaped everything. And through it all, God kept the doors open and a faithful remnant kept showing up.
2024
A Turning Point
Momentum begins to shift. Giving and attendance rise. Partners like Grace Point Chapel sacrifice to help the church find its footing. It is nothing short of a miracle.
2025
Matt Arrives
New leadership. New energy. New people every Sunday. The Holy Spirit is present and active. The church is growing — and that growth is real.
May 2026
The Elder Retreat — This Moment
Six leaders gather to ask what God requires of us now. The answer becomes the rallying cry for the next season: Strengthen Your Stakes.
Foundation Turning Points Now

He didn't want it to die. His hand is so evident on it. We have to tell the testimony.

Mike, Elder

The elders want the congregation to know this story. The survival and renewal of The Rock Church is not the result of great strategy — it is the evidence of God's faithfulness to a community that kept believing. And you have been part of that.

Our Values

What We're Building On

The elders spent significant time during the retreat naming what The Rock Church is built on — not just what we do, but who we are. These four values came out of that conversation. They are not new rules. They are who we want to be together.

One thing stood out above the rest: Intimacy with God is the foundation of everything else. When we are close to God, the other values flow naturally. They're not a checklist — they're the overflow of a community that genuinely knows Him.

The Foundation — Everything Flows From This

✦ Intimacy with God

"If 20 years from now that's what people said about us, I'm good." — Matt

◎

Truth & Authenticity

We say what we mean with kindness. We are real with each other. No pretending, no performing.

◇

Kingdom Mindset

We're not building our platform — we're building God's Kingdom. Humility, partnership, and generosity define us.

♡

Unconditional Love

We go into hard places with people. Love isn't conditional on comfort — it's a commitment we keep.

What this looks like for our team
  • We speak the truth to each other — with kindness and care, not avoidance
  • We celebrate wins beyond The Rock — when other churches and ministries thrive, so do we
  • We go after people who are far from God, not just those who are easy to reach
  • We stay close to God personally — ministry flows from that closeness, not the other way around

What We're Building Together

The elders identified five focus areas for the next six months. These aren't just leadership priorities — they are opportunities for every one of us to contribute to something bigger.

1
Vision & Story
Tell the Story — Cast the Vision
The story of what God has done at The Rock deserves to be told widely. And the 2045 vision deserves to be known by everyone in the building. When people know where we've been and where we're going, they invest with their whole heart.
2
Connection
Build Connection — Close the Back Door
New people are arriving every Sunday. We want every single one of them to find their people here. Building small groups, next-steps pathways, and genuine community is how a crowd becomes a church family.
3
Leadership
Develop Leaders at Every Level
The growth we're experiencing creates more opportunity than we currently have leaders to fill. We're investing in finding, developing, and releasing the next tier of serve team leaders, small group leaders, and ministry coordinators — that might be you.
4
Next Generation
Launch Student Ministry
We are committed to hiring a student pastor within six months. The next generation deserves a home at The Rock — a place to belong, grow, and be sent. This is one of the most exciting things we're building.
5
Team Health
Grow as a Healthy, Thriving Team
We're investing in becoming a staff team that has clear expectations, honest feedback, and a culture where everyone can grow and thrive in their role. A healthy team builds a healthy church.

The thematic goal is set centrally by the elders and carried by every person on the team. The Five Defining Objectives

These are the five pillars of the thematic goal. Every staff member's work connects to at least one of them.

1
Vision & Story
Present the Vision — Tell the Testimony
Communicating what God has done at The Rock and where He is taking us. The survival story needs to be told. The 2045 vision needs to be known. When people understand both, they give their whole heart to the mission.
2
Connection
Build Connection Infrastructure
Creating the systems and pathways that turn first-time visitors into connected members of the church family — small groups, next steps, assimilation, and follow-up processes that make sure nobody slips through the cracks.
3
Leadership
Develop the Leadership Pipeline
Identifying, developing, and deploying the next tier of serve team leaders, small group leaders, and ministry coordinators. Building the mid-level leadership layer that makes a growing church sustainable — and giving people a meaningful place to belong and contribute.
4
Next Generation
Launch Student Ministry
Hiring a student pastor and building a student ministry that gives the next generation a genuine home at The Rock — a place to be known, to grow in faith, and to be equipped for a life of following Jesus.
5
Team Health
Build a Healthy, High-Functioning Team
Developing the staff culture, clarity, and accountability that a growing church requires. Clear expectations, honest feedback, and an environment where every team member can thrive and grow in their role.
